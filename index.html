The flowering of the guayacanes is a phenomenon that occurs once a year and for a period of time of a few days.Once a year Ecuador lives a unique natural spectacle with the flowering of guayacanes, dry forest trees located in few areas of the country that are dressed in intense gold, perfuming the environment and embellishing the landscape and is planned for this weekend. These hardwood trees can grow up to 30 meters in height and two meters in diameter. The tree’s silhouette is wide, oval and globelike. The bark’s texture is rough. The tree crown diameter varies between three and four meters. It has palm-shaped leaves, big bell-shaped flowers, and cube-shaped fruits. These trees are yellow during the blooming season, dark brown in the defoliation season, and green when foliage is complete.

when?

The flowering of these trees occurs in January after the first winter rains of the year, which also announce the beginning of planting. The growth of the guayacanes is possible between 200 and 1,000 meters above sea level and for a tree to reach 15 meters in height it is necessary to wait up to 200 years.just once a year, an awesome phenomenon occurs – the guayacán trees come into full bloom. After a nine-month dry season, the rains begin, and trees that have sported bare branches for most of the year erupt in canopies of brilliant yellow flowers. It takes the guayacán trees about 200 years to reach a height of 50 feet, but in less than a week, they flower and then drop their petals, covering the ground with a carpet of bright yellow. Viewed from a hilltop above Mangahurco, the valley below is a magnificent sight, with yellow umbrellas stretching as far as the eye can see.

Enjoy

Horseback and cycling tours through the guayacán forests are available, and you can camp among the blossoming trees.

Visit

It’s vital to visit the area at exactly the right time. Remember, there is only a window of about a week to experience full flowering – and you don’t want to miss out…
